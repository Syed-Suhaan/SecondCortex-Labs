The Rotating Brain on the Hero
Here are three approaches, ranging from quick to premium:

Option A — Three.js (code, free, most control)
This is what's already partially set up in your codebase (BrainScene.tsx exists in your landing). You'd load a GLTF brain model and rotate it in a <canvas>. Best quality, fully customizable, matches your existing tech stack.
To get the model:

Sketchfab.com — search "brain low poly", filter by free + downloadable. Export as GLTF/GLB. There are several clean, stylized ones that would suit a dark UI.
Poly.pizza — free low-poly models, very clean aesthetic, download as GLB directly.

Then in Three.js:
js// rough shape of what BrainScene.tsx would do
loader.load('/brain.glb', (gltf) => {
  scene.add(gltf.scene)
  // slow Y-axis rotation in animation loop
  gltf.scene.rotation.y += 0.003
})
You'd apply a wireframe or emissive white material to match the monochrome palette.

Option B — Spline (no-code, free tier, very impressive)
Go to spline.design, import or find a brain model, add a rotation behaviour, then export as a <script> embed or React component. The output is a single line of code. Their free tier is solid for this use case.
This is probably the fastest path to something that looks premium. Spline has a community library with existing brain scenes you can fork.

Option C — Lottie animation (lightest, 2D)
If a flat illustrated brain with a rotation/pulse animation is enough, generate the illustration in Adobe Illustrator or Figma, animate it in LottieFiles, and embed with @lottiefiles/react-lottie-player. Extremely lightweight and crisp at any resolution.

My recommendation for your stack:
Since you already have BrainScene.tsx in secondcortex-web/src/components/landing/, the intent was always Three.js. Get a GLB from Poly.pizza (free, clean low-poly style), drop it in, apply a wireframe MeshBasicMaterial with white/grey color, and animate a slow Y rotation. It'll look exactly like the neural canvas already on the hero — but 3D and centered. Very coherent with the monochrome direction you just locked in.
